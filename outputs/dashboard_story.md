# Dashboard Story — Executive Leadership Briefing
## Retail Sales Performance 2024–2025

---

## Executive Summary

The business processed 4,200 orders across 2024–2025, generating ₹21.7Cr in sales and ₹3.33Cr in net profit at a blended 15.35% margin. Revenue grew 4.3% year-over-year. The headline numbers look healthy — but connecting the charts tells a different story underneath: **Technology is carrying the business**, **Furniture is diluting profits**, and **the discount policy has an uncontrolled margin leak** that worsens as order volume scales.

The dashboard reveals three concurrent stories: a growth story (Technology, South Region), a risk story (Furniture + high discounts + Furniture returns), and an opportunity story (East Region margins, Home Office segment, Social channel).

---

## What Is Performing Well

**Technology is the clear profit engine.** With 70.9% of total revenue (₹15.4Cr) and margins consistently above 18%, all four Technology sub-categories — Accessories (18.33%), Copiers (18.0%), Machines (18.07%), and Phones (18.49%) — perform well above the portfolio average. Copiers alone generated ₹4.06Cr in revenue and ₹73.1L in profit. The category also has the lowest return rate (3.03%), making it high-revenue, high-margin, and low-friction.

**The South Region leads volume with maintained margin.** Five southern states (Telangana, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh) combine for ₹6.47Cr in revenue and ₹99.9L in profit at a 15.44% blended margin. Telangana alone at ₹1.49Cr is the top state nationally. The South's lead is organic — it has both the order count (1,210) and the margin depth to sustain scale.

**2025 H2 recovered strongly from a weak 2024 summer.** August 2024 was the single weakest month (₹63L). By contrast, July–August 2025 averaged ₹107L — a 70% improvement. Year-over-year total sales grew 4.3% and profit grew 2.2%, with order count up from 2,057 to 2,143.

---

## What Is Underperforming

**Furniture is a structural margin drag.** At 6.9% average margin across its four sub-categories, Furniture sits 8+ percentage points below the portfolio average. Tables (5.67%) and Bookcases (5.71%) are the two lowest-margin products in the entire catalog. Furniture generates ₹5.16Cr in revenue but only ₹35.6L in profit — equivalent to what a single mid-sized Technology order might generate. Furniture is consuming proportionate shelf space, logistics cost, and customer service time for disproportionate profit return.

**Discounts above 20% are destroying value at scale.** The 21-30% discount band contains the most orders (1,086) and generates only an 8.05% margin. The 31%+ band (64 orders) is loss-making at -4.95%. Combined, approximately 1,150 orders — more than one in four — are being processed at margins that fail to cover typical operating overhead.

**First Class shipping costs are not being passed to customers.** First Class (1.77 avg days) generates the lowest margin of all ship modes (14.52%) vs Standard Class at 15.54%. Speed is being subsidized rather than monetized.

---

## What Risks Are Visible

**The Home Office return rate (5.67%) is the highest of any segment** and approaching the threshold where returns begin to materially erode the segment's revenue contribution. Home Office is currently the highest-revenue segment (₹7.45Cr), making a deteriorating return rate a high-stakes risk.

**Goa has an 8.1% state-level return rate** — the highest of any state (16 returns on 197 orders). This is either a product delivery or customer expectation issue specific to that market and warrants immediate investigation.

**288 loss-making orders are a systematic, not random, problem.** They cluster in high-discount Furniture orders — meaning they are predictable and preventable. At current trajectory, as Furniture volumes grow, so will the loss-making order count unless policy intervention occurs.

**The discount policy has no floor.** With 1,086 orders at 21-30% discount (the single largest order band), the business is organically drifting toward a discount-heavy sales model with each order cycle.

---

## What Opportunities Are Visible

**East Region's margin premium is an underexploited asset.** East has the highest blended margin (15.55%) but among the lower revenue totals (₹4.89Cr). Jharkhand at 16.83% and Assam at 16.22% are the highest-margin states in the country. Investing in Technology sales capacity in East states could unlock high-margin revenue growth with less pricing pressure than the South market.

**A 3pp margin recovery on 21-30% discount orders is worth ₹1.3Cr annually.** Simply capping discounts at 20% for non-priority orders and requiring manager approval above that threshold would, at current volumes, recover roughly ₹1.3Cr per year in margin that is currently being given away without strategic justification.

**Social channel acquisition has the best return quality (3.94% return rate)** at competitive margins (15.79%). Growing Social acquisition share from its current 13.9% of orders toward 20% would improve the blended portfolio return rate without sacrificing revenue quality.

**Furniture pricing experiment could add ₹2.5Cr to annual profit.** A 10% average price increase on Furniture (to achieve 16-17% margins) applied to ₹51.6Cr in Furniture revenue would generate approximately ₹2.5Cr in additional annual profit, assuming no significant volume decline. Given Furniture's captive market position (physical retail), moderate price increases often see lower demand elasticity than assumed.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

| Priority | Action | Expected Impact |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Discount cap: max 20% standard, 25% with manager approval | ₹1.3Cr+ annual margin recovery |
| 2 | Furniture pricing review: 10% price increase test on Tables & Bookcases | ₹2.5Cr potential annual uplift |
| 3 | Home Office return reduction program (enhanced descriptions + day-7 survey) | Protect ₹7.45Cr revenue segment |
| 4 | East Region Technology investment | Unlock 16%+ margin growth pocket |
| 5 | First Class surcharge increase or courier renegotiation | Close 14.52% → 15.5% margin gap |
| 6 | Furniture discount hard cap at 15% | Prevent loss-making Furniture orders |
| 7 | Goa returns audit | Reduce 8.1% state return rate |
| 8 | Expand Social channel acquisition budget | Lower blended return rate |

## Limitations of the Dashboard

1. **No cost breakdown beyond profit.** The profit field likely excludes some operating overhead (warehouse, returns logistics, customer service). True contribution margin may be lower than shown, especially for Furniture.
2. **Return costs are untracked.** The return_flag field identifies returns but not their cost. Furniture's 88 annual returns likely incur ₹150-300K in reverse logistics that are invisible in current margin calculations.
3. **Two-year window.** While sufficient for trend analysis, 24 months cannot identify multi-year seasonality or category lifecycle patterns.
4. **32 missing customer ratings and 24 missing channel tags** create minor gaps in customer rating and channel analysis reliability.
5. **No competitive context.** Whether discount levels are driven by competitive pressure or internal policy cannot be determined from this dataset alone.
6. **Delivery delay = absolute days, not relative to promised date.** An order delivering in 5 days may be on-time for Standard Class but late for First Class. True late delivery rates are not computable from this data.

## Suggested Next Analysis

1. **LTV cohort analysis** — do Furniture buyers have lower lifetime value than Technology buyers? Segment by first-purchase category.
2. **Return root-cause classification** — categorize return reasons (defect / wrong fit / changed mind) to target interventions specifically.
3. **Discount elasticity study** — compare sales velocity for same SKUs with and without discounts to determine true demand elasticity.
4. **State market share estimation** — overlay internal sales with external market size data to identify under-penetrated, high-potential states.
5. **Return cost quantification** — assign estimated reverse logistics cost per return to restate true Furniture profitability.
